Dora Andrade

A dancer who has transformed lives through dance.

Here a brief timeline of Dora Andrade’s life journey:

  • 1960: Dora Andrade is born in Fortaleza, Ceará. She begins her dance studies at the Hugo Bianchi Ballet Academy.
  • 1976 (16 years old): Dora founds her first dance school in Quixadá, Ceará, starting her journey as an educator and entrepreneur in dance.
  • 1980: Dora enhances her dance training by studying at prestigious institutions like the Guaíra Theater in Curitiba, the American School of Russian Classical Ballet in the USA, and the École de Danse Peter Goss in Paris.
  • 1980: She founds the Dora Andrade Dance Group, performing in various presentations and winning national and international awards.
  • 1991: Dora establishes EDISCA (School of Dance and Social Integration for Children and Adolescents) in Fortaleza, aiming to use dance as a tool for social inclusion and education for young people in vulnerable social situations.
  • 1995: Dora directs and presents the choreography "Jangurussu," which explores social and cultural themes from Brazil's Northeast region.
  • 2000: She co-directs the choreography "Duas Estações" with Gilano Andrade, and in 2003, creates and presents the production "Móbilis."
  • 2012: She launches the choreography "Só" in partnership with Gilano Andrade, bringing innovation and a contemporary approach to dance.
  • 2010s: Dora returns to her studies and completes an MBA in Social Entrepreneurship and Third Sector Management at the University of Fortaleza, seeking to improve the management of EDISCA.
  • 2024: Dora Andrade continues leading EDISCA, which has already impacted over 2,000 young people, being recognized both nationally and internationally for her work in social transformation and dance development.

Here is a summary of EDISCA's impact in numbers:

  • Over 2,000 young people have been served since its founding in 1991.
  • 350 students are attended annually, coming from underprivileged communities.
  • 850 meals are served daily to students.
  • 90% graduation rate from the full training program.
  • More than 1,000 artistic performances held in various countries.
  • 70% of the students served are girls, promoting female empowerment.
  • 1,500 psychological counseling sessions annually for students and their families.
  • 20 national and international awards received in recognition of social and cultural work.

"A arte é fundamental para o ser humano, é uma via privilegiada para educar. Não consigo entender uma educação de qualidade quando não é contemplada uma vivência consequente em arte."

-- Dora Andrade
